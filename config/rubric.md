# Einstein Moving — Sales Call Scorecard
**Source:** [x. Sales Call Scorecard](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Yd5JBiQLtEOxTUy1P_JF0N4Ua-UwM6vmiWxvhhh8ZGA)
**Total:** 85 pts (77 standard + 8 bonus). **Mastery = 77+ on 3 straight gradings.**

**Scoring rule:** Points are deducted if the manager fails to mention the bullet points required of the call. If the bullet point doesn't relate to the call, the point is automatically granted (N/A = automatic point).

---

## Section 1 — Set The Agenda (7 pts)

- [ ] Greet the customer
- [ ] Get customer's name
- [ ] "How are you today?"
- [ ] Recap the quote form / confirm move details on file (if applicable)
- [ ] Check availability for the move date
- [ ] Agenda statement — "I'll gather some details about your move, then walk you through pricing — takes about 10–15 minutes. Sound good?"
- [ ] "Sound good?" (explicit confirmation)

---

## Section 2 — Probe for Info / Pain Points (9 pts)

- [ ] Address and zip code (origin + destination)
- [ ] Home size (sq ft or bedrooms)
- [ ] Stairs / floors at origin and destination
- [ ] Additional rooms (garage, storage unit, balcony)
- [ ] Special considerations — long walks, narrow hallways, tight turns
- [ ] Packing needs — do they need packing services?
- [ ] Boxes — how many?
- [ ] Storage — any storage stops?
- [ ] Additional stops
- [ ] Access concerns — elevator, parking, HOA restrictions
- [ ] Moving stressors — "Is there anything you're particularly worried about with this move?"

---

## Section 3 — Pricing / Differentiators (20 pts)

- [ ] "Pricing is super simple" opener
- [ ] Hourly rate (per mover, per hour — with day-of-week tiers if applicable)
- [ ] What's included: gas, truck, taxes, insurance, wrap pads, bands, tools
- [ ] Disassembly / reassembly included
- [ ] Trip fee (if applicable — outside service zone)
- [ ] Packing service pricing (same hourly rate + materials)
- [ ] **No Worries Damage Coverage** — up to $2,000 per move to repair, replace, or reimburse accidental damages
- [ ] **Meet Your Mover** — email a few days before the move with mover photo + basic info
- [ ] **On-Time Guarantee** — if we miss the time window, we start discounting immediately
- [ ] Full "no hidden fees" pitch — no credit card fees, no transportation fees, no fuel surcharge, no assembly fees

---

## Section 4 — Clock (4 pts)

- [ ] Clock starts on-site when contract opens
- [ ] 2.5 hour minimum
- [ ] 15-minute prorated increments after the minimum
- [ ] Sense-check — "Does that make sense?" or equivalent

---

## Section 5 — Estimate (13 pts)

- [ ] Quote is accurate for the job described
- [ ] Typical time range for this home size
- [ ] Number of movers and trucks
- [ ] Variables explained:
  - [ ] Stairs / elevator
  - [ ] Specialty items (piano, safe, antiques)
  - [ ] Additional stops
  - [ ] Drive time / trip fee
  - [ ] Packing time (if applicable)
  - [ ] Walk distance
  - [ ] Equivalents used (school bus reference, etc.)
  - [ ] Traffic (if applicable)
  - [ ] Assembly / disassembly (if applicable)

---

## Section 6 — Save Your Ass (10 pts)

**The prep speech — this is the highest-value section for move efficiency.**

- [ ] Mention that prep before move day = shorter move = lower bill
- [ ] Consolidation examples: kids' toys, garden tools, loose items in drawers, photos on walls
- [ ] "Take a carload" tip — anything small can go in the car
- [ ] "Does that make sense?" or equivalent close

---

## Section 7 — Quote / Booking (11 pts)

- [ ] Attempt to set on schedule today ("We have availability — want to go ahead and lock that in?")
- [ ] No deposit required — mention explicitly
- [ ] Email handoff explained — "I'll send you a confirmation email"
- [ ] BOLDED list at the top of the email — confirm key details
- [ ] "Reply with any corrections" — customer verifies info
- [ ] "Any other questions before I let you go?"
- [ ] Hours and how to reach us

---

## Section 8 — Politeness (3 pts)

- [ ] Used customer's name 3+ times throughout the call
- [ ] Warm farewell
- [ ] Customer's mood improved or stayed positive by end of call

---

## Section 9 — Bonus / Helpfulness (8 pts)

- [ ] Speaking clearly and at good pace
- [ ] Efficiency — call was organized, didn't waste customer's time
- [ ] Ecobox or Sparefoot referral (if relevant)
- [ ] Made the customer laugh or built genuine rapport
- [ ] Effective insurance explanation (No Worries + 60¢/lb baseline)
- [ ] Addressed FAQs proactively
- [ ] Budget-conscious framing when needed

## Phone Presence Assessment (transcript-based, not scored against 85 pts)

**Scored separately on a 1–5 scale. Does not add to or subtract from the 85-pt total.**
**Important:** This is assessed from transcript language only — not actual vocal tone. Flag calls for audio review when the transcript gives insufficient signal.

### Rating scale

| Score | Label | What it looks like in transcript |
|-------|-------|----------------------------------|
| 5 | Outstanding | Genuine warmth, uses customer's name naturally, humor or rapport moments, customer responds positively (laughs, says "you're great", asks to work with this rep again) |
| 4 | Engaging | Consistently warm language, affirmations feel natural, customer seems comfortable, good energy in word choice |
| 3 | Adequate | Polite but transactional, warmth present but inconsistent, few affirmations, minimal rapport beyond the task |
| 2 | Flat | Mostly scripted/robotic, minimal acknowledgment of customer's emotions or concerns, little warmth in language |
| 1 | Disengaging | Curt, dismissive, or awkward — customer pushback goes unacknowledged, no name use, no empathy markers |

### What to look for in the transcript

**Warmth indicators (push score up):**
- Affirmations: "Absolutely!", "Great question!", "Of course!", "I totally understand"
- Empathy statements: "I know moving can be stressful", "That makes total sense"
- Genuine humor or lightness — customer laughs or reciprocates
- Natural transitions that acknowledge what the customer just said (not just plowing ahead)
- Customer explicitly expresses satisfaction, comfort, or asks to book with this rep specifically

**Flat/robotic indicators (push score down):**
- Mechanical transitions — jumps topic to topic without acknowledging customer response
- Scripted-sounding phrases repeated verbatim without variation
- Misses obvious emotional cues (customer expresses worry, rep ignores it)
- Barely any variation in language across the whole call
- Customer seems disengaged, monosyllabic, or asks questions that suggest they weren't listening

### Flag for audio review when:
- Score is 1 or 2 (low warmth — want to verify it's actually flat voice, not just sparse transcript)
- Score is 4 or 5 but customer seemed disengaged anyway (possible that transcript shows effort but audio tells a different story)
- Any call where the transcript doesn't give enough signal (very short exchanges, customer dominated, transcript gaps)

### Output fields to add to each graded call:
```
"phone_presence_score": 1-5,
"phone_presence_label": "Outstanding|Engaging|Adequate|Flat|Disengaging",
"phone_presence_notes": "One sentence — what drove the score",
"audio_review_flag": true/false,
"audio_review_reason": "Why flagged (or null)"
```
